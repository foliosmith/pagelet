# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

Participants are expected to collaborate respectfully, focus on technical
substance, and make space for maintainers and contributors to do careful work.

Examples of acceptable behavior:

- using clear and inclusive language;
- giving and receiving technical feedback constructively;
- respecting project scope and maintainer decisions;
- disclosing conflicts of interest when they affect a discussion.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- harassment, threats, insults, or personal attacks;
- sexualized language or imagery;
- publishing private information without explicit permission;
- sustained disruption of issues, reviews, or maintainer processes.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close threads, reject contributions, or block
participants when behavior harms the project. Enforcement should be
proportionate, documented when appropriate, and focused on preserving a
productive project environment.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns through the private maintainer contact channel
published with the repository. Reports should include relevant links, dates,
and context. Maintainers will handle reports with discretion and avoid
unnecessary disclosure.
